Johnathon Livingston Seagull

Published on 06 PM Friday 20 December 2013

book-review

A very light and enlightening read, indeed. There is so much in this book that make you just stop reading for a while and just think about stuff, about life, about stuff that matter in life, stuff worth doing and stuff you do just to make other happy.

This has to be the best motivational book I ever read. Absolutely amazing.

I love books like this, which tries to tell some very important life lessons indirectly. Take this for an example, you want to give some advice to some small kid, say something like "your time is very precious, don't waste it". Now you could either just say this directly to the kid, or you could cook up a short story based around a character who learns this lesson by the end of the story, and tell that story animatedly to that little child. The kid is more likely to respond to the latter for of advice than the former.

For example in the book, Jonathon is shown as thinking to himself the following lines, "Seagull discovered that boredom and fear and anger are the reasons that a gull’s life is so short,and with these gone from his thought, he lived a long ﬁne life indeed." Now this is such a simple yet a gem of a life lesson.

and when deciding what is wrong and what is right "“The only true law is that which leads to freedom,” Jonathan said. “There is no other.”"

Another example of such a book is "The little Prince" and "The Alchemist" by Paulo Coehello.

I remember when I was little, one day in the afternoon, I was playing outside with some friends of mine, we were playing a game in which we were pretending to play family-family. I was the father, another friend , she was the mother, and we got some other kids to be children,(lol, now that I think of it). So we were playing this game, and suddenly, I looked up and saw a formation of parachuters gliding down from sky, apparently some air drop exercise was being carried on that day. I kept looking at it in awe, it was something I had never seen before, and then I thought about the silly game I was playing with friends, family-family, and wondered while I play this silly game, people are doing awesome stuff like jumping from aeroplanes.

I got reminded of that incident while reading this book.
