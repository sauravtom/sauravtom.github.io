1984 by George Orwell


Published on 09 PM Monday 23 September 2013

book-review




Though a great book, with lovely quotes, I felt a bit disappointed with the last 100 pages or so, will probably have to read it again to enjoy that part as well. I didn't particularly understand the last part as to why was O'Brian being such a dick and Winston still regarded him as some sort of hero. And why was this double think ? How could he be good and bad at the same time.

I began reading the novel just because of the fact that most of the feed on /r/books was flodded with it, http://www.reddit.com/r/books/search?q=1984&restrict_sr=on

Here's what Issac Asimov had to think about the book. http://www.newworker.org/ncptrory/1984.htm

Here comes a candle to light you to bed,

Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two make four, if that is granted all else follows.
