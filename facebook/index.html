On Facebook


Published on 06 AM Tuesday 04 March 2014

general




For the past couple of months I've successfully managed to distance myself from facebook. Before I decided to do that I couldn't live a minute without checking my facebook wall feed.

To make this change, the first thing I did was that I degraded my facebook experience, I subscribed to some cringe worthy facebook people and pages so that my news feed looked like shit.Quite literally so, it was like, I would get up in the morning and check my facebook feed and my hands would get dirty and all with all the shit pouring our of my newsfeed.

That did had an effect on my facebook usage but only to a mild extent, a very mild extent. Despite all the friction created by my very talented facebook friends, I still was checking my facebook feed on hourly basis, in hope of a new notification.

It all had to do with notifications, these were like little pills of "soma" (The Brave New World reference) distributed throughout my day.

After that didn't work well, I made a rule of not posting anthing on facebook, until its extremely important. So as to minimize the notifications, and get me off of the "soma-notification" addiction.

Before I made this rule, I shared a lot of funny pictures on my facebook wall, with the intent of making people laugh, because I liked that.

But soon after that it became more about fishing for likes than anything else, likes became the seal of approval on my facebook posts, less likes meant not approved, more likes meant happiness. Soon I started engineering my facebook posts so as to fetch me maximum likes. All this hapenned subconsiously, ofcourse.

This optimisation proved to be lethal, with each successful post in this department, I got closer to facebook, I started loving facebook more and more.

But ever since I made this rule of not posting anything on facebook until it really important, life has been much better. My facebook usage and engagement is very down. As of now I only use facebook for

  • Keeping in touch with close friends with chat.
  • Finding about events in college.
  • Make really important facebook posts.

I still check on facebook every once in a while for the second point, because it sucks when I get to know about some interesting event in college only to find out that it was yesterday.

I also installed a chrome extension called "News Feed Eradicator" which is also quite helpful. What it does is that, it replaces your news feed with quotes about life and productivity.

I only come up with all this when I have a lot of free time, like in the holidays, otherwise when I am busy with something interesting, facebook doesn't bother me at all.

Facebook had become an addiction and it was necessary to put it in hold, so I made a set of protocols to deal with addiction of any kind.

  • Degrade the experience
    • For example if you are a cigarette addict, force yourself to smoke cigrattes which are bad in taste, deciding to smoke slightly worse cigratte will be far less easier than deciding to not smoke it at all.
  • Force yourself to not do it and then reward yourself.(with something other than doing the addictive thing)

I think a lot of similar protocols can be created like this, where some clever hack is being employed to indirectly make the user unable to attend to the addictive itch.

But the ultimate rule to cope with any sort of addiction is to just be mindful about things. Being mindful means being in the moment and have a clear consicience regarding what you are doing. "Mindfullness in Plain English" is a excellent book which teaches so.

But perhaps the best advice anyone can give you is a zen master,if you were to ask one, what is the best way to deal with addiction, they will simple reply

  • "Just stop doing it."
