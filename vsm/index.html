Virtual Stock Exchange


Published on 04 AM Saturday 01 March 2014

general




I woke up today and the first thing I saw was this image.

I had participated in a virtual stock market competition, which my college had organised. It was four days long competition, and we were given 10million dollars to start with, I joined in two days.

Let me tell you it was not at all an easy task, it was mostly luck.I made 98% of my wealth on my first day in the market out of two, and the other day was spent in losing 1% of it and then subsequently earning it back.

Shit went down last night, Apple stock was haywiring like anything and I lost quite a bit in that one and some other NASDAQ players, so yesterday after spending 4 hours trading and with just 40 minutes left for the bell, this was the situation.

I had lost all hope at this point,since I had lost a lot in the past 1 hour and as a result came down to position 4.

Everything I tried to gain some wealth resulted in more losses, and it had little to do with the state of market and more to do with my poor judgement.

Irritated by the simultaneous losses I made a impulsive decision, I sold all the stocks I had at that time and put them all in Apple. ALL OF THEM.

Apple stock was trading at 521 something at that time and I had a hunch that it was bound to go up and go up today itself, there was a risk too because had it gone up the next day then it would do me little benefit since this was the last day of the competition.

But luckily that didn't happen, you see at 3pm (EST) yesterday you can see a depression in the graph below, that was when I bought a ton of Apple shares.

Luckily for me the Apple stock went up and did went up that day only and closed 4 dollars higher.

And also luckily for me, other players were holding onto some stock which fell quite rapidly as the market started closed, and all of them suffered losses, and I came to first position.

I just got lucky with this think because I had virtual resources and could take big risks, but I seriously doubt that I will be able to replicate this sort of feat in the real world.Doing that would require lots of confidence which comes from studying stocks for years.

It basically boils down to how well you understand the shareholders of that particular stock, and how well you understand the herd mentality of people, and most importantly one should be able to take risks.

It intrigues me to think of people who do this for a living.

Like just imagine there are people out there whose daily routine is to wake up invest some in the market and then keep shuffling it till the evening and then at the end of the day take the money out and count it and make plans for the next day.

Its a vicious cycle.

But there are bright sides too, well there is lots of money to be made in there if you are smart enough and willing to learn says Warren Buffet, there are two rules in this game.

Rule No.1: Never lose money. Rule No.2: Never forget rule No.1. ~ Warren Buffet

Even though it was Virtual Stock market, the prize money was real. 10,000 ₹ (roughly $160 USD). With this and some meny saved up in the last couple of months, I finally will be able to buy a nice phone and ditch my present one.

Nah, I won't ditch it, I will probably keep it and sell it as an antique in 2063.

Needless to say, today was a good day.
