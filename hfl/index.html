Most important things in life ?


Published on 08 AM Sunday 11 May 2014

general




Some time ago, I asked myself the question, What is the most important thing in life ? And since this weekend happens to be the second last weekend before my exams begin, I got a lot of time to think over it, thanks to procrastination. Its amazing how much man can achieve while procrastination, but more about that later, I must first discuss my journey of finding the most important thing in life.

By life I mean, a life worth living, a healthy life in all aspects.

"The unexamined life is not worth living." ~ Socrates

And as Derek Sivers says in his amazing podcast, The Meaning of Life , so is the forgotten life.

This post is an exploration of things which should be important, if one needs to make their life more fulfilling and happy.

First thing, there is can be no single most important thing in life, there must be some things which are important in life.

So let's start with the most basic of things, health.

Health

It only after a injury, does one realise how important health actually is. Ask anyone in sports profession, and they will tell you how important it is. By good health I mean, physical and mental fitness as well.

Its not enough to just stay fit, its also vital to exceed ones limits and explore hidden muscle strands (by yoga and streching), or discover new moves (learning ancient fighting arts), and also dive deep into the subconscious (by meditation).

Physical Health can be maintained and made better by regular exercise. Thats a no brainer. It is also good for mental health too, regular exercise releases the hormone endomorphin which is critical to have a happy life, in some cases to stay sane even.

But apart from that, it apalling how less we do for our mental health.(does doing mental math puzzles count ?)

Meditation is a great way to not only stay mentally fit, but make mind sharper.

Its apalling how modern socienty lay absolutely no emphasis on mental health, just because its development is dormant. (as compared to physical health) I believe that just like we re-discovered physical health in the 80's and even hacked our way through it (using science), we(human civilisation) will re-discover mental health in meditation and may even find some cool hacks through it. Meditation basically simulates certain regions of the brain, which also can be done manually if one knows enough about brain. But that might take time, so it might be worth to learn meditation in the mean while. Also mechanical simulation will never be as fun a internal.
