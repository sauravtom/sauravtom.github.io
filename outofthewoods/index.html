Book review- Out of the woods by Chris Offut

Published on 06 PM Tuesday 30 July 2013

book-review

This is one of the 2 books I issued on my first visit to the American center in new Delhi, which is quite well known for its robust collection of books in the library (other book was Vanishing Point by David Markson).

This is a very special book, but a special book of a different kind. I am sure a lot of people would consider it a rather dull book. Even I did in the forst few pages, but I was wrong.

There are some books which a highly praised by a very small fraction of readers and yet somehow, have no special appeal to a large number of readers, and still they are special, this has to be one of them.

It is a collection of eight little stories, which tell very common tales, nothing out of the ordinary (for the most part). The tales mostly have characters hailing from Kentucky(USA), how they are rooted to their homeland even after having gone far from it. You will find the mention of kentucky in almost all of the stories. As a matter of fact, the author, Chris Offut's first books was called "Kentucky Straight". A quick look at then back of the book reveals that the author itself is from Kentucky,(no much of a surprise).

The Good Parts

  • It consists of short tales, so there is no question of getting bored, after every dozen a page, comes a new chapter with new characters and a new theme.

  • In between these mundane tales you will find vivid emotions, like the feeling you get when its 3pm on a bright afternoon, and its very quiet outside, and you just sit next to the window and listen to the song of the leaves. Those kind of emotions, you will find them scattered through out the books, and if you're not careful you might miss a few.

The not so good parts

  • Its not enlightening, its not thought provocative, its just lives of simple people , mundane and sometimes boring.(like imagine the exact opposite genre of scifi, and you've got a genre for this book)

Still , a nice read indeed, will give it 5.6 stars out of 10.
