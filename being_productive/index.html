On being productive

Published on 11 AM Monday 11 November 2013

general

One good thing about exams is that they act as stabalizers in our reactive teenage lives. You see in todays time, so much is going on in thw world, so much interesting things to do, so much to learn and so much to show off. All this is sometimes too much to handle, and if not handled carefully it leads to a burnout. Which is a very bad thing let me tell you that.

Exams on the other hand enforces a authority, creates pressure and makes life exciting. I love exams but only after they have ended, not before or during them. :p

Being productive is important, because it provides a better way to live life, and when there is a better way to do a thing then why do it any other way ?

Due to the current social media addiction, I have gradually , after spending countless hours online, trained my brain to recieve constant stimulus, one after another. If however I deprive my brain of this crucial stimulus, then it goes into a frenzy, and will keep at it until it finds that stimulus.

Thats why I have a hard time studying theoretical subjects from books. I am envious of my friends who are just opposite, for they can only study from books and have trouble consuming academic media in digital format.

My solution to this problem is to , retrain my mind to stay calm and focussed when I want it to be and not give it constant stimuli.

I installed this chrome extension called facebook unseen, and another one called facebook timeline purity, which basically replaces the facebook timeline with random quotes. Pretty neat stuff. After installing these, I can say that my life has changes quite a lot. Earlier I used to get periodic urges to check out my facebook timeline and see whats new and stuff. But now when I try to do that, I get a quote instead, and since the urge is not that powerful, I have no motivation to bypass that restriction and access the news-feed. BRILLIANT.

And the facebook unseen extension makes sure that noone gets to know whether I read their messages or not, hence making me less liable to respond to them.
