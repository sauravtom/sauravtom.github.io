Book Review- The Perks of being a wallflower by Stephen Chbosky


Published on 12 PM Monday 14 October 2013

book-review




I start reading it yesterday afternoon starting 2:32 and finished it today at 11:30 something. I had read a lot of great things about the book on /r/books, but frankly I was disappointed after reading the book. I couldn't fathom whom Charlie was writing all those letters to, and why did he got sick by the end of the book. I hope I will figure out by reading it again, but is it worth it ?
One thing I don't understand at all is that there are certain people who praise this book a lot, and would say things like I was depressed for a week after reading it and all, I don't understand how could this book have such a strong impact on someone.

And I think it should not at all be compared with "The Catcher in the Rye", for that is a book which belongs to a totally different class, not by genre but by sheer quality.

The things about Charlie's sexual abuse only became clear to me when I read about it here on goodreads, Or maybe I should watch the movie to get a better idea of the book.

I would rate it at a humble 5 out of 10, its a good book for teens nevertheless.
