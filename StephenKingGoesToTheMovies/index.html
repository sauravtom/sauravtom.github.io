Book Review- Stephen King Goes To The Movies


Published on 11 AM Saturday 15 June 2013

book-review




Stephen King goes to the movies is a decently fat book, just a little shy of 600 pages. For Stephen King , the experience of seeing his works in short fiction adapted for major hollywood films has created its own tales, and he shares them here in this book. Five of his celebrated short stories, including his introductions to the story as well as his take of the on screen adaptation of them, as well as behind the scene revelations. Which makes for a quite wholesome read. The five stories and short description, watch out for spoilers of course.

  • 1408 : Having seen the movie , I found this narrative quite disappointing to be frank, but then visual effects is a whole new ball game and it would be unfair to compare it with textual narrative. But despite that the story has its own charm, for here you are the director, the screen play writer, the special effects guys, and even the actor if you please to be.Covered in about 50 pages its a short little horror story.

  • The Mangler : Out of the five of the series, this one has to be the least charming. It a horror tale about a laundry machine that is haunted. I can't be the only one in this world who thinks that is a very naive build for a horror story.

  • Low men in yellow coat : This is the best out of five. Though the story is fiction, you only realize it about halfway through the three hundred and twenty pages long read. It got me nostalgic, but being one of the authors earlier works it lacks the emotional punch which makes you weep. You just don't, atleast I didn't. But nevertheless , a nice read, 5 stars.

  • The Shawshank redemption : This is a sort of summary of the novella Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption. And if you've seen the movie you will get a feeeling that it is an exact copy of the scrren play, because the movie is a very perfect onscreen depiction of the novel.

  • Children of the Corn : Confusing and wierd, little children running around in broad daylight chasing a truck driver who just ran over some 13 year old on the road.(though he was dead before he hit the hood of the truck). I read it and never got the chills you expect when reading Stephen Kings work. Dull and boring, though the movie based on it must be worth seeing, but the read, not so.
